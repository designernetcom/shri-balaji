Open schooling, as its name suggests, is open and welcomes all individuals seeking to begin their educational journey. This modern educational concept has redefined the way people approach learning, delivering a flexible and inclusive method of education. It differs from the traditional schooling conducted within the four walls of a physical school building with fixed schedules and structured curriculum.

The concept of open schooling traces its root back to the mid-20th century in response to the limitations of traditional educational systems. Traditional education models often left certain segments of the population, such as working adults, differently-abled individuals, and those living in remote areas, without adequate learning opportunities.

This lack of access to education was especially in developing countries, where economic, social, and geographic barriers blocked educational opportunities. The need for open schooling became increasingly evident in a world undergoing rapid transformation. To keep pace with these changes, governments took the initiative to introduce open schools. Their goal was to bridge this educational gap and ensure that learning remained accessible to a more diverse range of learners providing access to quality education for all.

In line with the initiative to make quality education accessible to all, the National Institute of Open Schooling (NIOS) was established by the Ministry of Human Resource Development (MHRD) in India in 1989. NIOS functions as an independent open school board, conducting secondary (10th) and senior secondary (12th) examinations that are on par with those by the CBSE and ICSE boards. It employs a student-centered approach through open and distance education, offering learners the flexibility to choose subjects according to their needs and study at their preferred pace.

The Ministry of Human Resource Development (MHRD) leads the effort to make open schooling an alternative to regular education, aiming to expand opportunities for disadvantaged and marginalized groups.

In this mode, it enables students to learn at their own pace and focus on their specific interests, strengths, and weaknesses. Open schooling promotes accessibility to education, particularly for individuals who face geographical barriers, physical disabilities, or with busy life schedules. It promotes diverse learning styles and caters to student’s unique needs.

To efficiently oversee the open schooling system, governments set up boards called open boards. These boards are managed by either the central or state government. Creating these open boards ensured that the quality of education in open schooling matched that of traditional institutions, by also providing primary, secondary, senior secondary, vocational, and skill education to diverse groups of students, with a strong focus on 10th and 12th.

3. BBOSE

Bihar Board of Open Schooling and Examination (BBOSE) was founded in February 2011 as an autonomous institution within Bihar's education department. It focuses on open and distance learning at the school level, combining elements of the State Council of Educational Research and Training and the Bihar School Examination Board. BBOSE offers unique features that set it apart from traditional examination boards and operates study centers in 38 districts across Bihar.

The Bihar Institute of Open Schooling (BIOS) provides a range of academic courses through open and distance learning, including:

  • OBE Programme (Open Basic Education)

The Bihar Board of Open Schooling and Examination (BBOSE) also extends academic courses through open and distance learning, offering diplomas and certificates for various programs with varying durations and qualification levels.

  • The minimum age for admission to BBOSE for class 10th is 14 years at the time of registration.
  • And for class 12th, one should be at least 16 years old.
  • There should be a one year gap between 10th and enrolling in the 12th.

Exam duration and blocks

  • Examinations occur twice a year for students enrolled in secondary, higher secondary, and professional courses via the Bihar Open Schooling and Examination Board.
  • Students who register during the first session, from March 1 to August 31, take their public examination in June of the following year.

4. WBCROS- The West Bengal Council of Rabindra Open Schooling

The West Bengal Council of Rabindra Open Schooling (WBCROS) is a government-administered board of education for distance learning. It was originally established as Rabindra Mukta Vidyalaya (RMV) in 1998 and later transformed into a statutory organization under the Rabindra Mukta Vidyalaya Act, 2001, which was subsequently amended in 2002 and February 2006. The latest amendment, in February 2006, resulted in its renaming as the West Bengal Council of Rabindra Open Schooling. WBCROS provides open learning opportunities through self-study materials and personal contact programs. It officially began its operations on January 1, 2007.

Courses

The courses offered by WBCROS include:

  • Madhyamik (Secondary) Course: This course is equivalent to traditional secondary education, with a duration of a few years. It provides students the opportunity to complete their secondary education.
  • Higher Secondary (HS) Course: The Higher Secondary Course is designed for students aiming to pursue higher education. It typically spans a minimum of two years and can extend up to five years, providing flexibility to learners.

Eligibility

Students should have completed the 10th and 11th grades from a recognized board with specified minimum marks. There is no upper age limit for admission, but candidates should be at least 16 years old.

Secondary (Class X) Course

  • Generally, candidates must have completed at least 9th grade from any certified board with the required minimum marks.
  • The minimum age limit for admission is typically 14 years.

Higher Secondary (Class XII) Course:

  • Eligibility criteria for the Higher Secondary Course may vary based on specific requirements set by WBCROS.
  • Rabindra Mukta Vidyalaya Candidates are assessed based on marks calculated out of 100 per subject.
  • For this course, there is no upper age limit for admission.

Special Eligibility Conditions for Higher Secondary Course Examination:

  • Learners who previously attempted the Higher Secondary Examination but did not pass within five years from the last examination date are eligible for admission.
  • Learners who appeared in the Higher Secondary Examination but did not pass in all subjects may benefit from credit transfer for some subjects. This credit transfer is valid for five years from the date of the last examination.
  • Those who have already passed the Higher Secondary Examination from other recognized Boards or Councils can enroll in WBCROS to study subjects not covered at the Secondary level. However, only a marksheet (no certificate) will be issued upon completion of the examination.

6. CGSOS- The Chhattisgarh State Open School

Founded in 2008, the Chhattisgarh State Open School (CGSOS) is a state-level autonomous institution empowered by the Chhattisgarh Government to deliver quality education using open and distance learning methods. With a forward-thinking vision, CGSOS provides an accessible and adaptable learning path. It serves as an alternative for individuals who, for various reasons, may have been unable to access traditional educational systems. Through open and distance learning, CGSOS empowers learners to pursue their academic and career goals, fostering a more inclusive and flexible educational environment.

Courses

The Chhattisgarh State Open School (CGSOS) offers various courses at the 10th and 12th-grade levels. These courses serve as a gateway for individuals to pursue education using open and distance learning methods, ensuring that quality education is within the reach of every student. While the specific course offerings may vary, here are some common types:

  • High School (10th) Courses: CGSOS offers High School courses, which are equivalent to the 10th standard in traditional education. Subjects encompass Mathematics, Science, Social Science, English, Hindi, and more.
  • Higher Secondary (12th) Courses: For those seeking to accomplish their 12th-grade education, CGSOS provides Higher Secondary courses, including streams like Science, Commerce, and Arts. These courses encompass subjects like Physics, Chemistry, Biology, Mathematics, Economics, and others.
  • Vocational Courses: Alongside traditional academic courses, CGSOS may offer vocational courses, designed to impart practical skills and knowledge in various trades, equipping students for employment opportunities.
  • Open Schooling: Open schooling programs cater to learners who, for various reasons, might have discontinued formal education. CGSOS provides them with the means to complete their education and earn recognized certifications through flexible study options.

Eligibility

For Class 10th:

  • Candidates have no specific age limit but must be at least 14 years old.
  • Successful completion of class 8th from a recognized board is required.

For Class 12th:

  • Candidates have no specific age limit but must be at least 16 years old.
  • Successful clearance of class 10th from a recognized board is necessary.

It's important to note that the eligibility criteria for the CGSOS board exam may change over time, and students are advised to refer to the official CGSOS website for the most up-to-date information.



Exam duration and blocks

  • The CGSOS board exams for both 10th and 12th classes occur in two blocks: one in March and April and another in September and October.
  • Examinations for both classes are held in a single shift, taking three hours.

10. UPSOSB- Uttar Pradesh State Open School Board
